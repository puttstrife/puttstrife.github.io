Palantir
Issue Management

Building a unified knowledge retrieval tool for IT service desk analysts — so the answer to any incident is one search away, not three tools deep.

2020 · UX Designer · Enterprise · IT Service Desk

The Situation

The knowledge existed. It just wasn't findable.

IT service desk analysts at Chi-Tech Philippines handle infrastructure incidents, resolve support tickets, and manage issues across multiple tracking systems every day — Redmine, JIRA, Confluence, and Shared Folders running in parallel.

When a server alert fired or a ticket came in, the analyst's first instinct was always the same: has this happened before? What was the fix? But finding that answer meant searching each system separately, with no way to know which one held the resolution — or if any of them did.

Years of institutional knowledge sat locked inside fragmented tools. Analysts were solving the same problems from scratch, escalating tickets that already had documented solutions, and burning time that should have been spent resolving incidents.

The Approach

A search layer, not a new system.

Research revealed the core workflow: incident arrives → analyst searches for prior resolution → applies it or escalates. The bottleneck was always step two. The solution wasn't another ticketing system — it was a unified search layer that sits on top of the existing ones.

Palantir queries Redmine, JIRA, Confluence, and Shared Folders simultaneously. Results are ranked by relevance and rated by helpfulness. Analysts upvote or downvote results to continuously improve accuracy over time — the system gets smarter the more it's used.

Filters let analysts narrow by market center, status, source, type, and date. Saved searches and recent search history mean repeated incidents are even faster the second time around.

The Results

Institutional knowledge, unlocked.

Palantir turned fragmented ticket history into a searchable, rankable, continuously improving knowledge base. Analysts find resolutions faster, escalate less, and stop solving the same problems twice.

Unified Search

One query across Redmine, JIRA, Confluence, and Shared Folders — no switching tools, no missed sources.

Relevance Ratings

Results ranked by helpfulness percentage. The more analysts vote, the more accurate the rankings become over time.

Smart Filters

Filter by market center, status, source, type, and date — narrowing results to exactly the context that matters for each incident.

Saved & Recent Searches

Persistent search history and bookmarked queries mean repeat incidents are resolved in seconds, not minutes.

Related Tickets

Every ticket surfaces related issues in the sidebar — giving analysts the full picture of a problem without additional searches.

Bookmarked Tickets

Analysts pin high-frequency incidents for direct access — reducing lookup time for the issues they see most often.

Conclusion

"The best IT tool isn't the one that manages the most tickets. It's the one that finds the right answer fastest."

Palantir puts institutional knowledge back in analysts' hands. When search becomes reliable, the entire support operation gets faster — fewer escalations, fewer duplicate resolutions, less time spent hunting across tools that were never designed to talk to each other.

The system improves with every search. That's the compounding advantage of a tool that learns from the people who use it most.
