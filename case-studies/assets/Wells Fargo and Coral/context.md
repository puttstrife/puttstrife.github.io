In this environment, a bad workflow doesn't just cost time,  it's a compliance liability.

I designed this system to eliminate that friction. Here's how.

In a regulated banking environment, direct user research wasn't possible.

So we worked with what we had — triangulating tasks, workflows, and proxy insights to make confident design decisions inside a closed system.

At that point, design stops being interface improvement. It becomes a business tool that helps the organisation operate more effectively




Boardroom:
"That's when design stops being UI and starts being infrastructure."

Tools:
Understand the Operational System — Miro or FigJam for system mapping, Lucidchart for more polished workflow diagrams. Microsoft-heavy org, Visio.
Identify the Critical Workflows — Miro for mapping flows, Excel or Google Sheets for logging task frequency and time-on-task data.
Gather Proxy Insight — Excel or Sheets for tagging and sorting support tickets, Notion or Confluence for organising notes from stakeholder sessions and training materials. If there's analytics, whatever the org uses — Google Analytics, Amplitude, etc.
Convert Insight Into Operational Artifacts — Excel for prioritised backlogs (impact vs risk matrix), Miro for service blueprints, Lucidchart or Visio for workflow diagrams. Confluence or Notion for the final documentation stakeholders actually read.
Align With Organisational Goals — PowerPoint or Google Slides for presenting alignment to leadership. Excel for mapping design decisions to business KPIs.
Short version: Miro, Excel, Confluence, and a presentation tool cover 90% of it. Figma only comes in when you're actually designing screens — which is the last step, not the first.

Who to navigate:
Understand the Operational System → Ops / Team Leads — Real workflows, not documented ones
Identify the Critical Workflows → Support / Service Desk — Top complaints and recurring issues
Gather Proxy Insight → Data / BI Team (Who owns analytics) — Usage analytics and behaviour patterns
Convert Insight Into Operational Artifacts → Product Manager — Priorities, roadmap, decision tools
	you've gathered the insights — now you need to check: does this align with what's already on the roadmap? What does engineering have capacity for? What does leadership care about this quarter?
Align With Organisational Goals → Compliance / Risk — What you can't touch
