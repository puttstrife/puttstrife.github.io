Asian Development
Bank

Designing a faster background-review workspace for loan assessment teams who need to understand risk, history, and supporting evidence before a case moves forward.

2019 · UX / UI Design · Banking · Risk Review

The Situation

The review process needed faster context.

Asian Development Bank teams need to identify the background of customers quickly, especially when loan applications involve complex history, bad records, or international criminal cases.

The problem was not a lack of data. The problem was the time it took to assemble the right pieces. Reviewers had to move between news, checklist pages, search views, and entity references before they could make a confident decision.

For a high-risk financial workflow, that delay compounds. Every extra step increases uncertainty and slows down the review queue.

The Approach

One workspace for the case, the evidence, and the notes.

The redesign combines a news view, checklist workflow, related-entity references, and supporting evidence into a single review flow. Instead of splitting context across disconnected screens, the reviewer can stay inside one environment while moving from discovery to validation.

Checklist states make progress visible. Inline WYSIWYG notes support faster annotation. Attachments and quicklinks keep supporting material close to the decision, while charted entity children and search views help reviewers trace the background of a case without losing their place.

The Design

Landing page with a news tab and project overview — a quick entry point for reviewers who need recent context before opening a case.

Dashboard search surfaces customer and project context fast, so the team can validate history before moving deeper into the review.

Checklist pages show answered states clearly, helping reviewers track what has already been verified and what still needs attention.

Inline review tools keep notes attached to the case, so analysts can annotate and validate without leaving the workflow.

Attachments and supporting documents stay attached to the review, so evidence is always close to the decision being made.

The Results

Background checks became easier to follow.

The new flow gives reviewers a cleaner way to move from search to checklist to evidence. That reduces friction in a process where confidence matters more than speed alone.

Faster context assembly

News, search, and checklist data live in one flow instead of forcing reviewers to reconstruct the case from scratch.

Clearer risk evidence

Attachments, notes, and entity references stay connected to the review, making the rationale easier to trace.

Less back and forth

Reviewers can move through the workflow without jumping between disconnected tools for every decision.

Better case visibility

Checklist states and charted relationships make it easier to understand where the case stands at a glance.

Conclusion

"When risk review is clear, the whole decision chain moves with more confidence."

The Asian Development Bank concept focuses on making background review legible. The goal is not to hide the complexity of the case, but to organize it so reviewers can act on it faster and with more confidence.
