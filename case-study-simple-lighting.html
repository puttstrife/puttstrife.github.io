Simple Lighting

Redesigning a large UK B2B lighting platform so it works better on mobile, removes legacy clutter, and makes product exploration feel lighter.

2024 · UX / UI Design · B2B Commerce

The Situation

Built for desktop. Left behind on mobile.

Simple Lighting is a major B2B platform in the UK, but the experience had grown heavy over time. The legacy interface worked well enough on desktop, yet it did not translate cleanly to smaller screens and needed a clearer hierarchy for busy customers.

The goal was to reduce visual noise, make the browsing flow easier to scan, and keep the platform functional for users who jump between desktop and mobile while comparing products and sections.

The Approach

Two core screens, one visual system.

The redesign focused on stronger spacing, a clearer rhythm between sections, and a more mobile-aware structure. Instead of forcing every interaction into one rigid desktop composition, the layout was adapted into a system that could breathe on smaller screens.

The Results

More clarity, less friction.

The updated design gives Simple Lighting a more modern, mobile-aware presence without losing the practical density a B2B platform needs. The comparison screens show the UI shift directly, and the rest of the flow keeps the broader experience intact.

Mobile-friendly layout

The interface is structured so it scales more naturally to smaller screens without breaking the content hierarchy.

Cleaner information flow

Section spacing and hierarchy make the content easier to understand at a glance.

Motion-led storytelling

The two comparison screens show the old and new UI side by side so the redesign reads as a direct comparison.

Legacy reduced

The redesign moves away from the older heavy UI language toward something lighter and more current.

Conclusion

"A B2B platform does not have to feel heavy to be serious."

Simple Lighting shows that practical enterprise content can still feel modern. The redesign focuses on clarity, motion, and hierarchy so the experience works for real users who need to move quickly across devices.
